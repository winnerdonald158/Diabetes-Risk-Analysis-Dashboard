                                              Diabetes Risk Analysis Dashboard
Basic Excel cleaning , PostgreSQL and Apache Superset analysis of patient health data to understand patterns associated with higher risk of diabetes.

                                            Project Overview
This project focuses on analyzing patient health data to identify patterns and trends associated with diabetes risk.
I used Excel for data cleaning, PostgreSQL for data analysis, and Apache Superset for visualization. I explored patient demographics, BMI, HbA1c levels, and other health indicators to uncover meaningful insights.

The goal of this project is:
i.To  understand diabetes distribution,
ii.To identify high-risk patient groups,
iii.To  highlight key health indicators linked to diabetes

The Tools I Used:
Excel, PostgreSQL and Apache Superset

The Skills Demonstrated:
Data cleaning, data aggregation, CASE statements, calculated columns, health data analysis, data visualization, dashboard design, analytical thinking

Business Objective:
To understand the overall distribution of diabetes among patients and to identify key factors contributing to higher risk;
•	I analyze how diabetes is distributed across patients 
•	Identify demographic patterns among age groups and gender 
•	I evaluate the relationship between BMI and diabetes 
•	Examine HbA1c levels across different patient groups

 Key Findings:
Diabetes Distribution:
A significant portion of patients fall into diabetic and pre-diabetic categories, indicating a notable health concern.

BMI and Diabetes Risk:
Patients classified as diabetic tend to have higher average BMI compared to non-diabetic patients.
This suggests  that there is a strong relationship between body weight and diabetes risk.

HbA1c Levels:
Diabetic patients show higher HbA1c levels, while non-diabetic patients maintain lower levels.
This confirms HbA1c as a key indicator of diabetes.

Age Patterns:
Older age groups show a higher concentration of diabetic cases compared to younger groups.
Age is a contributing factor to increased diabetes risk.

Gender Distribution:
Both male and female patients are affected, but the distribution helps identify if one group has slightly affected more than the other.

Insights / Recommendations:
•	Patients with higher BMI should be monitored closely as they show increased diabetes risk,
•	HbA1c levels are a strong indicator and should be prioritized in early diagnosis,
•	Older patients may require more frequent screening,
•	Preventive healthcare strategies should focus on lifestyle and early detection 

Key Takeaway:
This project shows that diabetes risk is strongly influenced by measurable health indicators such as BMI, HbA1c, and age.
Understanding these patterns can help in early detection and better health decision-making.
